About Me

University of the Philippines, 2014-2019

I graduated from the University of the Philippines with a degree in Business Economics. Since economics requires the analysis of data sets, I was “forced” to study tools such as Stata and R. This is to make sense of data and provide high-quality interpretation, with the hopes of contributing to significant local and national policy changes. Circa 2018, I started delving into programming by studying Stata and R. This was necessary for me to complete my undergraduate thesis at the time.

FactSet, 2020

During my stint at FactSet, I worked as an Advisor for Client Solutions, wherein I provided support to clients regarding financial markets in the United States. Answering questions regarding the stock market and the different stock prices, dividends, valuation, and trends were part of my daily routine. With this, I could apply the skills that I have acquired in my degree. However, something seems to be lacking on my end, knowing that this is not what I want to do in the long run.

Cyzerg, 2021

After resigning from FactSet, I worked as a Marketing Supervisor for Cyzerg. As part of my work, I created articles for the company targeted towards several buyer personas in the warehousing industry. Also, I single-handedly managed the company’s social media platforms and came up with ideas on how to increase traffic to our website and ultimately boost conversion rates. Moreover, I was part of the team in-charge in creating a new website for the company. With this, I was able to work first-hand with the graphics and the development team. Around this time, my desire to learn programming has been reignited, having seen the inner workings of creating a website.
